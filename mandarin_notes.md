## Time

### Structure of Time

```
      hour    minute
       |         |
  [7 diǎn   15 fēn]
       |         |
   "the th"   "fourth"
              (un)
```

**Example:**
- 7 diǎn 15 fēn → Sì diǎn shíwǔ fēn : 7 hour 15 min

> When we have 10 o'clock → only "shí diǎn"

**5 hour, 50 minutes → 5 diǎn 50 fēn**

---

## Time Question Word

**To ask "What time is it?"**

```
jǐ diǎn jǐ fēn
  |         |
"what hour"  "which minute"
  |         |
 high       low
```

- `jǐ` = "its not wh-, its X" (with low tone)
- `jǐ diǎn jǐ fēn` = what hour, what minute

## Negative Word: bù/bú (不)

> **bù** turns statements into negative statements. (Also: "Boouh")

| Usage                        | Rule                                              |
|------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| bù + [4th tone word]         | bù becomes bú (tone 2)                            |
| bù + [other tones]           | stays bù                                          |

### Examples

```
(A) → bú è    (so go high, one dot)  (buwoo) — not hungry
(B) → bú rè                          → not hungry
(C) → bú lèi → not feeling hot
```

> **bù pronunciation:**
> - Longer when with 4th tone
> - Shorter & sharper when with other tones

---

## CONTD. — Negative Sentences / Words

> The negative word **bù** has 2 tones.
> When combined with a word of 4th tone, its tone is tone 2.

Examples:
```
bù máng, bù kě, bù lěng
```

**Negative word is pronounced as bú when paired with any tone apart from 4th one.**

## ma (吗) — Question Particle

> **ma** → A word that turns sentences to yes-no questions.

---

## How to Form Questions

We either do:
1. **Verb + bù + Verb** (e.g., rè bú rè = hot or not hot?)
2. **Statement + ma?** (e.g., rè ma? = Is it hot?)

---

## 们 men — Plural Marker

> **们** (men) — AKA "mun" — means plural. Emphasis on "n".

```
wǒ men → We all
nǐ men → You all
tā men → They all
```

- `bù` → when next word is 4th tone
- `bù máng` → when next word is 2nd tone

## Ordering Phrases

```
请 (qǐng)  给 (gěi)  我 (wǒ)  [topic]
(Please)  (give)   (me)
```

### Examples

```
Please give me 2 colas:
qǐng gěi wǒ liǎng bēi kě lè

→ Sān shí wǔ kuài = $35 price

→ 1 hamburger:   yī gè hàn bǎo
→ A glass of cola: yī bēi kě lè
→ 2 hamburgers:  liǎng gè hàn bǎo
   (we don't use èr while counting with measure words)

→ How much is 1 hamburger:
   yī gè hàn bǎo duō shǎo qián?
   (Question words always come last. So price? will always be at last.)
```

## yi (一) as Measure Word

> When next word is NOT 1st tone → yi becomes **yí**
> When next word is tone 1 → ex. bēi

# Textbook Practice — Page 23

## Q&A Frames

### What do you like to eat?

```
你喜歡吃什麼?
nǐ xǐ huān chī shén me?

A. 奶茶 (nǎi chá) — milk tea
B. 可乐 (kě lè)   — cola
C. 汉堡 (hàn bǎo)
```

### What book are you reading?

```
書 看 我 你 英文 什麼
shū kàn wǒ nǐ yīng wén shén me

① nǐ kàn shū shén me?
   nǐ kàn shén me shū?
② wǒ kàn yīng wén shū
```

### What car are you buying?

```
車 買 我 你 日本 什麼
chē mǎi wǒ nǐ rì běn shén me

① wǒ mǎi rì běn chē
② nǐ mǎi shén me?
   nǐ shén me chē mǎi?
   nǐ mǎi shén me chē?
```

### zhēn de / kāi wán xiào de

```
開玩笑的 (kāi wán xiào de)
→ "Just Joking" (sounds like Jensen but last)

真的 (zhēn de)
→ It's true

他們吃漢堡 → zhēn de
他們很累   → kāi wán xiào de

Is it true?: zhēn de ma?
```
